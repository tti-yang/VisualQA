Aliasing ghost (N/2 ghost)

A faint, displaced copy of the brain shifted by half the field of view along the phase-encode direction. On EPI (functional and diffusion) it appears as a shadow of the head offset by half the image, often visible in the background but sometimes falling across brain tissue itself.

The call, in short

  • Ghost falling across the central brain → the affected signal is non-physiological. Exclude.
  • Faint ghost confined to background, not overlapping tissue → note it, check whether it worsens across the run, and check whether your ROI is near the affected region.
  • Re-window before deciding. Narrowing the top of the display window brings faint out-of-brain signal into view; a ghost invisible at the default window can still bias intensity-based metrics.
  • Ghosting on every scan from one session, site or coil → suspect hardware or reconstruction rather than the participant, and raise it with the scanner physicist.
  • Check the background, not just the brain. Patterns outside the head are where ghosts, spikes and scanner problems announce themselves.

Severity ladder

AppearanceCallReasoning
No visible ghost at a narrowed display window Include Nothing to act on.
Faint ghost in background only, no overlap with tissue Uncertain May still shift intensity-based metrics. Check GSR and whether it worsens across the run.
Moderate ghost, possibly with other artifacts present Uncertain Degree of signal effect is hard to judge visually. Follow up with interactive inspection before deciding.
Strong ghost crossing central brain regions Exclude Affected voxels carry summed signal from two locations; correlation patterns there are non-physiological.

Metrics

MRIQC reports Ghost-to-Signal Ratio (GSR), which estimates mean signal in the regions prone to N/2 ghosting along the phase-encode axis, relative to mean signal inside the brain mask. Lower is better. GSR makes ghosting sortable across a dataset, so it is an efficient way to triage which scans to inspect visually — but it is a summary number and does not tell you where the ghost lands, which is the part that determines whether the scan is usable.
