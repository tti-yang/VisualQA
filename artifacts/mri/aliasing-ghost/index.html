Aliasing ghost (N/2 ghost)

A faint, displaced copy of the brain shifted by half the field of view along the phase-encode direction. On EPI (functional and diffusion) it typically appears as a shadow offset by half the image; it is easiest to spot when the display window is narrowed so low-intensity signal outside the brain becomes visible.

The call, in short

  • Faint ghost confined to the background, not overlapping brain tissue → usually tolerable; note it and check whether it worsens across the run.
  • Ghost overlapping brain, or bright enough to shift voxel intensities inside the head → corrupts the signal where it lands; flag for exclusion or correction.
  • Always re-window before deciding. A ghost invisible at the default window can still bias intensity-based metrics.
  • Ghost present on every run from one session or one coil → suspect hardware or reconstruction, not the participant; raise it with the scanner physicist.
