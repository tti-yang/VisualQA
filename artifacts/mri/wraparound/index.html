Wrap-around (aliasing from an undersized FOV)

Anatomy lying outside the field of view along the phase-encode direction is folded back into the image, appearing superimposed on the opposite side of the head. Unlike an N/2 ghost, the wrapped tissue is at full intensity and lands directly on top of real anatomy.

The call, in short

  • Wrapped tissue confined to background or non-brain regions → cosmetically ugly but often usable; document it.
  • Wrapped tissue overlapping brain → those voxels contain summed signal from two locations and cannot be separated after reconstruction. Treat affected regions as unusable.
  • Check whether your region of interest is in the overlap. A wrap landing on cerebellum matters differently than one landing on your target ROI.
  • Unlike motion, this is a prescription error, not a participant behaviour. If it appears, check the protocol before the next participant is scanned — it will recur on every scan until the FOV or phase-encode direction is fixed.
  • Registration and normalization can fail silently on wrapped images, so re-inspect downstream outputs even if you decide to keep the scan.
