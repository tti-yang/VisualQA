Artifact Detection and Rejection

A beginner-friendly reading list for recognizing, rejecting, repairing, and correcting EEG artifacts. Each entry explains what the paper adds and which part is most useful to read first.

New to this topic?

Start with Islam et al. (2016) for the broad overview, then read Zhang et al. (2024) for a practical test of correction and rejection. Use the remaining papers when you need a specific method.

2000 Dense-array EEG/MEG

Statistical control of artifacts in dense array EEG/MEG studies

Junghöfer, M., Elbert, T., Tucker, D. M., & Rockstroh, B. Psychophysiology, 37(4), 523–532.

What it explains: A statistical workflow for finding contaminated trials and channels in dense-array recordings, with channel interpolation used to preserve otherwise usable trials.

Look at: The step-by-step rejection criteria and the examples showing why a few bad sensors should not force rejection of an entire trial.

2010 Saccades & gamma

Saccadic spike potentials in gamma-band EEG: Characterization, detection and suppression

Keren, A. S., Yuval-Greenberg, S., & Deouell, L. Y. NeuroImage, 49(3), 2248–2263.

What it explains: Why tiny saccades can look like neural gamma-band activity, how the spike potential appears across the scalp, and how it can be detected and reduced.

Look at: The waveform and scalp-topography examples, the detection results, and the tailored ICA procedure.

2010 Automated pipeline

FASTER: Fully Automated Statistical Thresholding for EEG artifact rejection

Nolan, H., Whelan, R., & Reilly, R. B. Journal of Neuroscience Methods, 192(1), 152–162.

What it explains: An automated pipeline that applies statistical thresholds at several levels, including channels, epochs, independent components, and participant averages.

Look at: The pipeline overview, the measures used to flag outliers, and the comparison with supervised cleaning.

Free repository PDF DOI
2011 ICA classification

ADJUST: An automatic EEG artifact detector based on spatial and temporal features

Mognon, A., Jovicich, J., Bruzzone, L., & Buiatti, M. Psychophysiology, 48(2), 229–240.

What it explains: An automatic method for labeling ICA components associated with blinks, eye movements, and generic discontinuities.

Look at: The spatial and temporal features used by the classifier and the examples of artifact versus neural components.

Free PDF DOI
2015 Filtering before ICA

On the influence of high-pass filtering on ICA-based artifact reduction in EEG-ERP

Winkler, I., Debener, S., Müller, K. R., & Tangermann, M. 2015 IEEE EMBC, 4101–4105.

What it explains: How the high-pass filter applied before ICA changes decomposition quality and downstream artifact reduction.

Look at: The comparison across filter cutoffs and the outcomes for signal-to-noise ratio, classification accuracy, and near-dipolar components.

2016 Recommended first read

Methods for artifact detection and removal from scalp EEG: A review

Islam, M. K., Rastegarnia, A., & Yang, Z. Neurophysiologie Clinique, 46(4–5), 287–305.

What it explains: A broad map of EEG artifact types and the main families of detection and removal methods, including their strengths and limitations.

Look at: The method-comparison sections and tables. They are useful for choosing an approach based on artifact type rather than assuming one method works for everything.

2017 Automated repair

Autoreject: Automated artifact rejection for MEG and EEG data

Jas, M., Engemann, D. A., Bekhti, Y., Raimondo, F., & Gramfort, A. NeuroImage, 159, 417–429.

What it explains: A cross-validation approach that learns data-driven thresholds for rejecting bad trials and repairing bad sensors instead of relying on one manually chosen cutoff.

Look at: The difference between global and local autoreject, how thresholds are learned, and the before/after examples.

Free PDF DOI
2024 Practical evaluation

Evaluating the effectiveness of artifact correction and rejection in ERP research

Zhang, G., Garrett, D. R., Simmons, A. M., Kiat, J. E., & Luck, S. J. Psychophysiology, 61, e14511.

What it explains: A practical framework for testing whether artifact procedures reduce both condition-related confounds and random noise. It evaluates ICA blink correction plus extreme-value trial rejection across five common ERP components.

Look at: The two goals of artifact handling, the evaluation workflow, and the component-by-component results showing that correction helps but may not remove a confound completely.
